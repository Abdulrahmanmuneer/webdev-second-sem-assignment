Habitat Destruction and Coastal Development

Rapid coastal development, including the construction of infrastructure, resorts, and hotels, leads to the destruction and degradation of nesting beaches for turtles. Activities like sand mining and pollution from construction also contribute to habitat loss.

Consequences: Loss of nesting sites can disrupt the breeding and nesting behavior of turtles, reducing their reproductive success. It can lead to population decline and even local extinctions.

Prevention: Implement and enforce strict regulations on coastal development, particularly in sensitive turtle nesting areas. Promote sustainable tourism practices that minimize the impact on turtle habitats. Conduct regular surveys to identify and protect important nesting sites.

lllegal Wildlife Trade

The illegal wildlife trade targets Sri Lankan turtles for their eggs, flesh, shells, and live specimens. Turtle eggs are gathered and sold, causing turtle numbers to fall. Captured and smuggled live turtles are used in the exotic pet trade.

Consequences:Illegal trading places enormous strain on turtle populations, potentially leading to species extinction. Turtles' natural reproductive cycle is disrupted when eggs are harvested.

Prevention: Strengthen law enforcement efforts to combat wildlife trafficking. Increase penalties and raise awareness about the illegality and detrimental impacts of the trade. Support community-based conservation programs that provide alternative livelihoods for local communities dependent on turtle exploitation.

Fishing and Bycatch

Turtles are mistakenly caught as bycatch in fishing nets and lines. Destructive fishing methods, like as trawling and dynamite fishing, increase the danger of turtle entanglement or injury.

Prevention: Promote the use of turtle-friendly fishing gear and techniques, such as TEDs (Turtle Excluder Devices) in fishing nets, to reduce bycatch. Enforce regulations on fishing practices and establish marine protected areas where fishing is restricted or regulated. Conduct education and training programs for fishermen on the importance of turtle conservation and bycatch mitigation.
